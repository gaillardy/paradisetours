The Story Behind The Six Triple Eight

The Six Triple Eight movie recounts the remarkable story of the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion. This all-Black, all-female unit played a crucial role during World War II.

Their mission was to sort millions of backlogged mail for soldiers fighting in Europe. Despite facing discrimination, these women delivered hope to countless servicemen and their families. The movie portrays their courage and determination.

The 6888th Battalion exemplified resilience. It highlights the untold contributions of African American women during the war. Learn more about their history.

The Cast and Crew: A Stellar Line-Up

Directed by Tyler Perry, The Six Triple Eight features a powerful cast. Kerry Washington leads the film, bringing depth and emotion to the story.

Oprah Winfrey and Viola Davis also play key roles, showcasing their unmatched talent. The cast ensures authenticity and emotional impact.

The production team prioritized historical accuracy. They consulted historians and descendants of the 6888th Battalion. This attention to detail honors the legacy of these remarkable women.
